# Module 02 · Structure · 结构方法论

> Stage 5. Pick a structural spine and lay the beats. Methodologies are tools, not
> dogma — they can be mixed (Story Circle for the skeleton, Save the Cat beats for
> detail). Choose by genre and intent.
> 结构是工具不是教条，可混用。按题材与意图选型。

---

## §1. Selecting a methodology · 结构选型

| Genre / intent | Recommended | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial genre (action, thriller, comedy, horror) | **Save the Cat** 15 beats | beats timed to the minute; strict rhythm control |
| Growth / adventure / transformation | **Dan Harmon Story Circle** | the 8-step ring fits "leave home → trial → return" |
| Character-complex, theme-first drama | **McKee Story Principles** | value-charge and unity-of-opposites; deep drama |
| Character-driven art film | **Inner-beat table** + a style variant | beats are inner-state turns, not external events |
| Mixed / unsure | ask preference; default Story Circle skeleton | universal and legible |

State the recommendation and the reason; let the user confirm before expanding.

## §2. Save the Cat — 15 beats · 商业片节拍

Each beat has a time window; the film runs like a rhythm machine.

```
ACT I (~25 min)
 1  Opening Image      旧世界，与终场画面对照；本身必须是一段值得看的视听内容
 2  Theme Stated       ~5min，他人无意道出主题，主角尚不懂
 3  Set-Up             1–10min，日常、缺陷、世界；让观众在乎主角
 4  Catalyst           ~12min，打破平衡、不可逆的事件
 5  Debate             12–25min，主角的犹豫——Flaw 让他抗拒改变
ACT II-A (~30 min)
 6  Break into Two     ~25min，主动选择进入新世界
 7  B Story            承载主题论点的关系线；A 问"能否达成"，B 问"能否成长"
 8  Fun and Games      30–55min，预告片段落；用旧方法应对新问题
 9  Midpoint           ~55min，A/B 交汇，伪胜利或伪失败，赌注升高
ACT II-B (~30 min)
10  Bad Guys Close In  55–75min，内外压力同时收紧，Flaw 开始有代价
11  All Is Lost        ~75min，最低点，"死亡气息"，旧方法彻底失败
12  Dark Night         75–85min，独自直面 Lie 与 Ghost；弧光转变临界
ACT III (~15 min)
13  Break into Three   ~85min，用全新方式想通；A/B 认知融合
14  Finale             85–105min，五步高潮：集结→执行→高塔惊魂→挖掘内心→新计划
15  Final Image        与开场对照，新常态；一个画面胜过千言
```

## §3. Dan Harmon Story Circle · 故事圆环

A character leaves the comfort zone, enters the unknown, pays a price for what
they take, and returns changed. The ring's beauty: "back to the start, but
different."

```
1 YOU     舒适区中的角色常态（开场仍须是精彩视听内容，非平铺交代）
2 NEED    意识到 / 被迫面对自己缺什么
3 GO      跨过门槛，不可逆地离开舒适区
4 SEARCH  在新环境中试错、遇盟友与阻碍（≈ Fun and Games）
5 FIND    得到想要的（≈ Midpoint），但代价随之而来
6 TAKE    为所得付出代价，圆环底部，旧自我开始瓦解
7 RETURN  回到熟悉处境，但已不再适合
8 CHANGE  新常态，与第 1 步对照
```
Symmetric (3↔7 go/return, 4↔6 search/take), looser than STC, ideal for journeys.

## §4. McKee Story Principles · 故事原理

Story is **the turn of value**. Every scene must flip a life-value (love/hate,
freedom/bondage, truth/lie) irreversibly. A scene with no value turn is a dead scene.

- **Controlling Idea** — the theme as one provable judgment: `[value] + [because]`.
  ("Justice wins, because the protagonist sacrifices all they hold dear.") It
  decides the ending.
- **Value Charge** — each scene flips its core value (+→− or −→+). Same charge at
  end as start? Cut the scene.
- **The Gap** — the rift between expected and actual result of an action. The wider,
  the stronger. Good scripts deepen the gap each time.
- **Inciting Incident** — shatters the balance; creates the central question, unanswered
  until the end.
- **Progressive Complications** — escalate, each failure costlier; the *inner* plight
  must deepen alongside the outer.
- **Crisis** — a true dilemma (least-worst, or irreconcilable goods); both options
  must have weight.
- **Climax** — the crisis choice enacted, irreversible.
- **Resolution** — the new order; the Controlling Idea proven or disproven.

## §5. Inner-beat table + style variants · 文艺片内在节拍

For character-driven film: beats are inner-state turns. The external trigger can be
tiny (a glance, an offhand line, a walk) but it sets off an internal earthquake.

```
1 Normalcy & crack    日常，但一个细节暗示表层之下的不安（开场须代表全片气质）
2 Disturbance         一件小事打破角色的自我欺骗
3 Drift & avoidance   试图回到原状，大量"间隙时刻"——独处、发呆、回避
4 Confrontation       被迫面对一直回避的东西（常是一场安静的对话）
5 Choice or acceptance 高潮是"接受了什么"而非"做了什么"
6 Aftershock          结局是开放的新问题，改变已发生但后果未至
```

**Composable flow profiles** (describe craft dimensions rather than imitating a
named living creator; mix deliberately):

- **Daily-observation flow 日常观察** — long dwell, the crack accumulates through
  repeated routine, ending on an ordinary action newly charged with meaning.
- **Mood-association flow 情绪联想** — scenes link through emotion/motif as well as
  causality; intentional VO/image friction; an emotional turn may replace a
  conventional climax.
- **Still-life flow 静物空间** — fixed architectural frames and transitional space
  images; social politeness carries conflict; acceptance rather than victory.
- **Conversational drift 对话游移** — digression/return rhythm; apparent small talk
  accumulates subtext; a precise line or silence can be the decisive turn.
- **Genre-hybrid flow 类型混合** — a clear dramatic skeleton carries tonal/genre
  shifts; space and props accumulate social meaning across the turn.

When a user names a reference, extract the requested dimensions (tempo, framing,
information release, dialogue density, tonal contrast) and build a distinct
combination for this project; do not copy signature scenes or phrasing.

## §6. Foreshadow & payoff planning · 伏笔与回扣（长片/剧集）

Plant in the front; pay off later with greater meaning. Layers: prop / line /
behavior / structural. Rules: the first appearance must not read as a plant; every
plant needs a payoff (an unpaid plant is a loose end); the payoff should re-color
the audience's understanding of what came before. Track with the setup register:

```
Setup | Layer | First appears | Payoff at | How the payoff changes meaning
------|-------|---------------|-----------|--------------------------------
```
Feature: 3–5 pairs across layers. Series: per-episode + short-range (1–3 ep) +
long-range (5+ ep) + a season-level setup paid at the finale.
