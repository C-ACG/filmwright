# Direction · Visual Language · 视听语言（景别 · 机位 · 运镜 · 构图 · 光）

> The director's grammar. A written scene says *what happens*; this module decides
> *how the camera makes the audience feel it*. Every choice here must be **motivated**
> — by story, emotion, or POV — never decoration.
> 导演的语法。剧本写"发生什么"，本模块决定"镜头如何让观众感受到"。每个选择都必须有动机。

---

## §0. The one rule above the rest · 镜头动机第一

**Motivation before technique.** Before choosing any shot, answer: *whose feeling
is this, and what does the audience need to understand right now?* The shot serves
that answer. A beautiful shot with no motivation is noise. When in doubt, the
simplest shot that serves the beat wins.

A useful chain for every beat:
`What is the dramatic action? → Whose POV anchors it? → What must the audience
notice? → Therefore: size, angle, movement, cut.`

---

## §1. Shot size · 景别

How much of the subject fills the frame. Size controls **psychological distance**:
the wider the frame, the more context and the colder the read; the tighter, the
more intimacy and pressure.

| Code | 中文 | What's in frame | Typical use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **EWS / ELS** | 大远景 | landscape; figure tiny or absent | establish place, scale, isolation |
| **WS / LS** | 远景 / 全景 | full figure + environment | geography of a scene, blocking, power of space |
| **MLS** | 中远景 | knees-up | group dynamics, movement within a space |
| **MS** | 中景 | waist-up | the default conversational shot; gesture + face |
| **MCU** | 中近景 | chest-up | intimacy without claustrophobia; the "interview" frame |
| **CU** | 近景 | face fills frame | emotion, decision, the line that matters |
| **ECU / BCU** | 特写 / 大特写 | eyes, hands, a detail | reveal, micro-expression, a prop's weight |
| **Insert** | 插入 | an object / detail, no actor | a clue, a text, a trigger |

**Working principles:**
- **Cut to size for meaning.** A push from MS to CU on a line means "this line
  matters." Hold the wide when the *space* is the point.
- **Don't over-tighten early.** Open a scene wide enough to orient; save the CU as
  a payment the scene earns.
- **The ECU is a scalpel.** It forces attention. Spend it on reveals and turns.

## §2. Angle & height · 机位与角度

Where the camera sits relative to the subject encodes **power and alignment**.

| Angle | 中文 | Reads as |
|---|---|---|
| Eye-level | 平视 | neutral, equal — the honest default |
| High / down | 俯拍 | subject is small, vulnerable, judged, trapped |
| Low / up | 仰拍 | subject is large, dominant, heroic, threatening |
| Bird's-eye / top | 顶视 | fate, pattern, dehumanization, "seen from above" |
| Dutch / canted | 斜角 | unease, instability, a world out of joint |
| Over-the-shoulder (OTS) | 过肩 | binds two people in a conversation; whose shoulder = whose POV |
| POV | 主观 | we see exactly what the character sees |

**Height = staging power.** In a confrontation, who looks up and who looks down is
the scene's subtext rendered in geometry. Reverse the height across a scene to
mark a power flip — the loser was shot from above, the winner now is.

## §3. Camera movement · 运镜

Movement is the most expressive and most expensive choice. **Static is also a
choice** — and often the stronger one. Move the camera only when the move *means*
something.

| Move | 中文 | Made by | Reads as |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pan | 摇（水平） | pivot on axis | follow / reveal across space; link two things |
| Tilt | 俯仰 | pivot vertically | reveal scale up or down; a body, a tower |
| Dolly / track in–out | 推 / 拉（轨道） | camera moves on a line | push = intensify, draw us in; pull = isolate, release |
| Track / follow | 跟（横移） | camera parallels the subject | sustained motion, "with" the character |
| Crane / jib | 升降 | camera rises/falls | god's-eye lift; emotional swell or diminishment |
| Handheld | 手持 | operator's body | immediacy, chaos, documentary truth, anxiety |
| Steadicam | 斯坦尼康 | stabilized rig | fluid travel through space; the oner's workhorse |
| Zoom | 变焦 | lens, not camera | flattens depth; an artificial, often unsettling pull |
| Whip pan | 甩 | violent fast pan | energy, transition, comedic snap |
| Push-in on a turn | 情绪推 | slow dolly during a realization | the canonical "she just understood" move |

**Distinctions that matter:**
- **Dolly ≠ zoom.** A dolly changes our spatial relationship to the subject (we
  move with them); a zoom changes magnification (the world flattens). They feel
  different — choose deliberately.
- **The oner (long take / 一镜到底)** earns realism, in-the-room presence, and the
  irreversibility of time — at the cost of coverage flexibility. Use it where
  *unbroken time* is the point.
- **Stillness as device:** in a high-emotion beat, a locked frame can be heavier
  than any move. Let the actor move inside a still frame.

## §4. Composition · 构图

The arrangement of the frame. Composition directs the eye and encodes relationship
before a word is spoken.

- **Rule of thirds 三分法** — place subjects and horizons on the third-lines, eyes on
  the upper third. Centered framing reads formal, ritual, or unsettlingly symmetric.
- **Headroom & lookroom 留白** — leave space in the direction a character looks or
  moves; deny it to make the frame feel oppressive.
- **Depth / layers 纵深与层次** — foreground / midground / background. A second action
  in the background annotates the foreground without a cut (in-frame juxtaposition).
- **Frame within a frame 框中框** — doorways, windows, mirrors box a character →
  entrapment, surveillance, separation.
- **Lines & balance 线条与平衡** — leading lines pull the eye to the subject; a
  deliberately unbalanced frame creates tension that resolves when balance returns.
- **Negative space 负空间** — emptiness around a figure = loneliness, smallness, the
  weight of what's absent.

**Blocking-into-composition:** the actors' positions *are* composition. Two people
at opposite edges of a wide frame, a third between them in the background — the
geometry tells the audience the alliance before the dialogue does.

## §5. Lens & focus · 镜头与焦点

- **Focal length 焦距:** wide lens (short) exaggerates depth and space, distorts at
  the edge, makes rooms feel large and faces dramatic up close; long lens
  (telephoto) compresses depth, isolates a face from its background, flattens a
  crowd into a wall.
- **Depth of field 景深:** shallow (背景虚化) isolates the subject — emotion, intimacy,
  subjectivity; deep (全清晰) keeps environment legible — context, simultaneity,
  the world pressing in.
- **Rack focus 变焦点/拉焦:** shift focus from one plane to another within a shot to
  *redirect attention* without cutting — from a face to the thing behind it that
  changes everything.

## §6. Lighting intent · 光（意图，不是布光图）

This module specifies *what the light should mean*, not a gaffer's plot.

- **Key / fill / back 主光 / 补光 / 轮廓光** — the three-point basis; the ratio between
  key and fill sets the mood (low fill = high contrast = drama/threat; high fill =
  flat = safety/blandness/clinical).
- **Motivated light 动机光** — every light should appear to come from a source in the
  world (window, lamp, screen, fire). Motivated light is invisible; unmotivated
  light is "lit."
- **High-key vs low-key 高调 / 低调** — high-key (bright, low contrast) for comedy,
  romance, safety; low-key (dark, hard shadows) for noir, threat, mystery.
- **Practicals 实用光** — lamps, neon, phone screens visible in frame; they motivate
  exposure and add production value cheaply.
- **Direction & color 方向与色彩** — backlight = mystery / power / silhouette; top
  light = judgment; side light = inner division; a color shift (warm→cold) can
  mark an emotional or temporal turn without a line.

## §7. Mapping emotion to grammar · 情绪 → 语法速查

A starting palette, not a formula. Always re-derive from motivation (§0).

| The beat wants… | Reach for… |
|---|---|
| Intimacy / a decision lands | push to CU/ECU, shallow focus, still frame, motivated soft light |
| Power / threat | low angle, long lens compression, negative space above, low-key |
| Vulnerability / entrapment | high angle, frame-within-frame, wide lens room-as-cage |
| Chaos / immediacy | handheld, fast cuts, Dutch angle, whip pans |
| Loneliness | EWS, negative space, locked static, cold practicals |
| A reveal | rack focus, insert, push-in, or a held wide that lets the audience find it |
| Tension building unbroken | the oner — Steadicam through space, no cut to relieve |
| Calm before the break | symmetric composition, slow or no movement, deep focus |
