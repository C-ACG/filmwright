# Format · Concept Ultrashort · 概念超短片（1–3 分钟）

> Pure idea delivery. The weapon is not story — it is the sharpness of the concept
> and the punch of the audiovisual language. Loads `00` + `01`.

## Traits
Runtime 1–3 min · characters are functional archetypes (or none) · usually no arc
(at most a one-instant cognitive flip) · plug-and-play world legible in 5s · zero
dead frames · audiovisual form *is* content. Two paths, different from step one:

- **What-If** — posit a counter-intuitive premise, show its consequences and a
  twist. Weapon: concept purity + combination (`01-development.md` §1).
- **How-to-Tell** — re-tell a known thing through an unexpected narrative form.
  Weapon: the form itself.

## What-If structures · 结构（不限三段式）
- **A · Three-part** — concept (15–30s, show the rule, don't explain) → escalation
  (30–90s, 2–5 consequence slices, each more extreme) → twist (15–30s, a single
  image/line that re-defines everything; the twist is an extension of the concept's
  own logic, never a hard turn).
- **B · Progressive collapse** — rule → escalation → escalation → collapse point →
  afterimage (the quiet ruin).
- **C · Parallel juxtaposition** — two lines run in parallel (split-screen / cross-cut
  / fg–bg); the audience *realizes* they're two faces of one thing — that realization
  is the twist, unspoken.
- **D · Loop** — A → develop → seem-resolved → back to A; the second A reads
  completely differently.
- **E · Reverse reveal** — show the result first (an unreadable image), then unwind
  the cause; the final reveal re-colors the opening image.

## How-to-Tell methods · 形式创意
Perspective swap (a bullet's POV of a war) · format mimicry (a marriage as a
product manual; a life as a search history) · time surgery (a lifetime in 2 min; a
1-second action stretched to 3) · scale jump (cell → galaxy → cell) · rule
constraint (one fixed shot, no cut; no humans, only objects; sound only, black
screen). The form must be *inseparable* — if removing it leaves the story intact,
change the form.

## Audiovisual weapons · 视听语言武器库
Ultrashort is the legal space for formal experiment. Pick **1–2** core devices and
push them to the limit; never stack five. The device must escalate (single→quad
split; forward→reverse) and can become the twist itself (full film color → cut to
b&w). See `modules/direction/visual-language.md` for the grammar; here the device
*is* the content.

Lens: oner · fixed-frame time-lapse · continuous dolly · first-person POV · macro.
Edit: jump-cut montage · split-screen · cross-cut · reverse · freeze.
Frame: symmetry · fg/bg in-frame narrative · color limitation.
Sound: image/sound mismatch · amplified ambience · silence.
Media: screen-recording · mockumentary · data-viz.

## Workflow (3 steps)
1. **Concept forging** — output 3–5 concepts as `one-line / combination method /
   real anxiety it points at / escalation potential / twist potential / suggested
   structure / suggested AV device`. Test: one sentence? 5-second visual? real
   social emotion? ≥3 escalating consequences? twist potential? fits in 3 min (else
   downgrade to short)?
2. **Structure & AV design** — pick a structure (A–E), 1–2 devices, expand to a
   timed outline with pacing tags.
3. **Full script** — write it all at once; minimal or zero dialogue; the twist on
   the last image/line; specify the AV concretely ("the camera starts on her hand,
   pulls back slowly to reveal the whole room — every workstation is empty").

## Traps
needs explanation → change/downgrade · escalation is repetition not progression ·
hard twist with no logical link · cramming an arc into 3 min · dialogue
"explaining the concept" · device unrelated to concept · too many devices ·
combination with no chemistry (1+1 must be >2) · form is mere packaging.
