# Template · Treatment · 一页纸故事大纲

> The stage-2 deliverable for feature (one page, 300–500 字) and the season synopsis
> for series. A treatment reads like the experience of the film, in prose, present
> tense — not a beat list. Its *tone* must telegraph the film's genre and feel.

## Shape

```
TITLE 片名

LOGLINE (one sentence)
who + wants + opposed by + at the cost of + under what pressure

TREATMENT (300–500 字, present tense, prose)
  ¶1  The world and the protagonist in their normal — the cross-section we cut into,
      and the crack already showing.
  ¶2  The catalyst that breaks the balance; the want that drives them out the door;
      the first real obstacle. If there is a B-story, let its presence be felt here.
  ¶3  The midpoint turn — the false win or false loss — and the rising cost. The
      Lie is tested.
  ¶4  The shape of the climax (don't over-specify) and the emotional key of the
      ending — the new normal, what it costs, what it leaves.

THEMATIC ARGUMENT (feature) 主题论点
  A contestable proposition; A-plot and B-plot take opposite sides.
```

## Rules
- **Prose, not beats.** A treatment is felt, not parsed.
- **Tone is content.** A reader should finish it knowing *what kind of film this is*.
- **No dialogue, no scene numbers.** Save those for the outline and script.
- **Revisable.** It is a draft of the whole, not a contract.

## Series variant · 剧集季度梗概
Same shape, scaled to the season: the season premise as one logline, then a
one-page prose synopsis of the season's trajectory and tone, followed by a
one-line logline per episode. Full planning structure in `series-bible.md`.
