# The Last Shift · Development (stages 1–4) · 开发

## Stage 1 · Core dramatic action · 核心动作

```
Protagonist 主角：MEILING (50s), a night-shift convenience-store clerk
Want 外在需求：get through the shift and the sale of the store without breaking down
Conflict 阻碍：the homeless regular she's been quietly feeding is the father who
              walked out on her family twenty years ago — and he's about to recognize her too
Logline：A night-shift clerk selling her late mother's store must decide whether to
         acknowledge the homeless regular she feeds nightly — the father who abandoned
         them — before the store, and the last thread between them, is gone by morning.
```

## Stage 2 · Synopsis draft · 梗概草稿

> 美玲在母亲留下的便利店上最后几个夜班——店铺下周易主。每晚打烊前，她都给一个常来的
> 流浪老人留一份关东煮，从不多问。今晚老人盯着新换的"旺铺转让"招牌看了很久，叫出了她
> 的名字。她认出了他：二十年前抛下她和母亲离开的父亲。一夜之间，她要在愤怒、亏欠和那
> 份从没说出口的想念之间，决定在天亮、店关、两人最后一根线断掉之前，要不要认下他。
>
> Tone: quiet, cold-blue, restrained — an art-leaning short. The drama is internal;
> the plot stays loose so the emotion can run heavy.

## Stage 3 · Character & arc · 人物

```
MEILING
  Want 外在需求：finish the sale, keep it together
  Need 内在欲望：to stop punishing herself for a wound that was never hers to carry
  Arc 弧光：armored caretaker who serves at a distance → a person who lets one
            person back across the counter (A → B)
  Bio 小传关键点：raised the store after her mother got sick; never married; feeds
            strays, human and animal, but never speaks to them
  Contradiction 矛盾：endlessly generous in action / completely walled-off in contact
  (Hinted Ghost: the night her father left, she was the one who locked the door
   behind him — and has kept doors half-open ever since.)

KAI / the father — function: catalyst + mirror
  Want：a glimpse of the life he forfeited, without asking to be forgiven
  Contradiction：came back every night for months / never once stepped inside
```

## Stage 4 · Backstory & world (cross-section) · 前史与世界观

```
Era / context：a fading neighborhood; chain stores closing the small ones; the
  "for lease" sign is the era pressing in.
Protagonist backstory：father left when she was 12; mother ran the store alone, then
  with Meiling; mother died last spring; the sale closes next week.
Relationship history：father and daughter, no contact for 20 years; he has been a
  nightly regular for ~3 months — neither has acknowledged the other.
World rules：realist, no genre conceit. The "rule" is economic: this store, and the
  ritual it holds, expires on a date.
Cross-section choice 横截面选择：we cut in on the last shift, the night the sign
  changes — the one night the unspoken thing can no longer stay unspoken, because
  after it the place that held them both is gone.
```

> Backstory delivery: none of the above is narrated. It diffuses into the apron, the
> half-open door habit, the changed sign, and one stopped hand.
