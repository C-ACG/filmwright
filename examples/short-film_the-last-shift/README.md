# Example · "The Last Shift" · 《最后一班》

A complete walk-through of Filmwright on a 10-minute short film, from seed to a
shot-broken scene. Each file is a real stage deliverable, using the system's
templates.

一个完整的范例：把 Filmwright 用在一部 10 分钟短片上，从种子到拆好分镜的场景。

---

## The seed · 种子
> A convenience-store clerk on the night shift realizes the homeless old man who
> keeps coming in is the father she lost contact with years ago.
> 上夜班的便利店店员，发现常来的流浪老人其实是失联多年的父亲。

Format: short film (5–10 min) · language: bilingual notes, Chinese script.

## The files · 文件（按流水线顺序）

| Stage | File | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | [`01_development.md`](01_development.md) | core action, synopsis, character, world |
| 5 | [`02_beat-sheet.md`](02_beat-sheet.md) | four-part structure + runtime + pacing |
| 7 | [`03_scene-04.fountain`](03_scene-04.fountain) | the pivotal scene, in Fountain |
| 7d | [`04_scene-04_shot-list.csv`](04_scene-04_shot-list.csv) | that scene's director breakdown |

## How to read it
Start at development and follow the pipeline. Note how:
- the **world is built before the structure** (stage 4 precedes stage 5);
- the beat sheet carries a **runtime budget and dual-track pacing** per beat, with a
  deliberate plot-loose / emotion-heavy mismatch on the pivotal scene;
- the Fountain scene contains **no interior narration** — the recognition lands on a
  stopped hand, not a "she realized";
- the shot list gives the **turn** its only camera move and its silence, and lets
  the **listener** carry the moment.

This is a demonstration of the method, not a finished film — the doctor stage (8)
and the remaining scenes are left as the natural next steps.
